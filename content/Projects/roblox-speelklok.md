**Role:** Art direction, 3D world building, gameplay previsualization, and coordination of 3D artists. For **Broken Egg** (immersive studio).

**Museum Speelklok** in Utrecht is the largest museum for music machines in the world. Medieval church full of centuries-old musical clocks, street organs, music boxes, automata. They wanted to become the first Dutch museum to launch a game on Roblox. My job was to turn that wish into a world.

## From brief to visual pitch

The agency handed me a text description of what the game should be. No concept art. No references beyond "it's a museum about music machines." So I built clay render animations of every mini-game before a single line of game code was written. Piano key pavements you jump on to make music. Pinball on giant music box metal plates. Springs that crush you if you don't dodge.

![[Speelklok_screeshot_A003.webp]]

![[roblox-clayanim001.gif]]

These previews became the decision-making tool. The client and Broken Egg could see what each mini-game would feel like, approve or kill ideas, and give the game developer a clear visual target. Saved weeks of back-and-forth.

## Building the world

![[Speelklok_screeshot_A004.webp]]

Once the dev started building, I shifted to world construction. Two islands: Music Island and Motion Island. The environment is inspired by Utrecht itself. The Dom tower, the canals, the medieval architecture. All reimagined as a Roblox world where 8-year-olds run around collecting musical notes and fighting a monster that used to be a brilliant inventor.

![[roblox-gif-2.gif]]

Coordinated a team of 3D artists to build the environments, textures, animations, and all the details that make a game world feel alive. Every building, every prop, every material.

![[Speelklok_screeshot_A007.png]]

## The result

**Music Machine Monster** launched as a free Roblox game. Nine missions, single and multiplayer. **The game reached 351k visits**. First Dutch museum to have it's own adventure on Roblox.
